Tiago Macedo

I am an assistant professor of mathematics at the Federal University of São Paulo (Unifesp), in Brazil. In the past, I have been a post-doctoral fellow at the University of Ottawa (in Canada), an assistant professor at the Federal University of Goiás (UFG-RC), and a Ph.D. student at the State University of Campinas (Unicamp). During the post-doc, my supervisor was Alistair Savage, and during the Ph.D., my advisors were Adriano Moura and Daniel Nakano (University of Georgia).

I am currently involved in two research projects related to representation theory of Lie algebras and superalgebras. I am also teaching an undergraduate level and a graduate level class at Unifesp. More detailed information about me can be found in my Curriculum Vitae and in my Currícu­lo Lattes.

The best way to contact me is via email at tmacedo@unifesp.br.

Research

My research interests lie in representation theory of Lie algebras, superalgebras, hyperalgebras, and algebraic groups. I am particularly interested in studying characters and extensions of modules, block decompositions, centers of enveloping algebras, and automorphisms of Lie algebras.
