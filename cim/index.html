Communications in Mathematics

Technical editing process

Step 1

As soon as an author sends their manuscript, verify if the manuscript is in the format of the journal.

  • If it is not, then ask the author to edit their manuscript using the format of the journal. Here is a template email that you can use:
    Dear author,

    Thank you for your response.

    The template we use to publish articles in Communications in Mathematics is
    attached. Notice that there are 3 files. The only file you will need to
    edit is commat.tex. However, you will need to have all of these 3 files
    inside the same folder in order to successfully compile file commat.tex.

    Please, send us the edited commat.tex file together with any other source
    file that is necessary to successfully compile your article as soon as
    possible.

    Best regards,

    <your name>
    Technical editor of Communications in Mathematics
    Remeber to attach: commat.tex, commat.cls and episciences.png.
  • If the manuscript is in the format of the journal, proceed with its revision (Step 2).

Step 2

Revision of the paper. Make sure to check the following:

  • Spell and grammar. My suggestion if that you copy the content of the paper, open Google Mail, create a new email and paste the content of the paper. Google checks both spell and grammar.
  • Sentences starting with a mathematical formula. My suggestion is that you search for ". $" in the content of the paper.
  • Avoid inline formulas longer than half of a line. Use math displays \[...\] instead.
  • Always use the enumerate and itemize environments for lists.
  • Always use \dots, \ldots, \dotsc, \cdot, etc, instead of "..." or ".". My suggestion is that you search for "..." in the content of the paper.
  • Instead of numbering equations or citing a reference by hand (using parenthesis or brackets), always use \cite, \ref and \eqref.
  • Delete all horizontal or vertical spacing, such as \hskip, \vskip, \smallskip, \medskip, \bigskip, \\, \newpage and \clearpage.
  • The references contain: authors' names, title, no empty fields (such as empty parenthesis), the page numbers are not followed by a dash (replace "--" by "-" between the pages' numbers).
  • Add the following lines right before "\begin{paper}":
    \VOLUME{<volume number>}
    \YEAR{<volume year>}
    \NUMBER{<issue number>}
    \firstpage{<first page of the paper: odd>}
    \DOI{https://doi.org/10.????/cm.<id of the paper>}
  • Add the following line right before "\end{paper}":
    \EditInfo{<received date>}{<acceptance date>}{<handling editor>}

As soon as you are done reviewing the author's manuscript send it back to the author (Step 3).

Step 3

As soon as you are done reviewing the author's manuscript send it back to the author, asking if there is any further changes they would like you to make. Here is a template email that you can use:

Dear author,

Thank you for sending your manuscript.

I am attaching to this email an updated version of your it. If there is
anything you would like us to change, please let us know.

Best regards,

<your name>
Technical editor of Communications in Mathematics

Make sure you attach the pdf file of their manuscript to this email.

Step 4

When the author and you agree with all the edition of their article, send the source files that you used to compile the final version of their article to them. Here is a template email that you can use:

Dear author,

Thank you for your response.

In that case, I would like to ask you to update your posting on ArXiv/HAL
to its final version (see attached files). Please, do not make a new
posting, as EpiSciences uses the id of the posting in the open repository
that was informed during submission.

As soon as your article is updated, please, let me know.

Best regards,

<your name>
Technical editor of Communications in Mathematics

Make sure you attach the tex file of the final version, together with: commat.cls, episciences.png and any other file (figures, etc) that the author may need to successifully compile the tex file.

General suggestions

  • Be kind and patient.
  • Try to perform all the steps within 24hs. In the beginning, Step 2 may take a little longer. But it gets easier and faster with time.
  • Never change the file commat.cls.
  • Do not ask the authors to perform more than one action in the same email.
